Smart Cities

  • Often about control only
  • Increase "efficiency"
  • Top down
  • Ignores legacy of urbanism
  • Little benefit to people
  • No challenge to status quo
  • Increase inequality / assimetry

Outside of the usual smart cities narrative

  • How to relate with existing urban infrastructure, not only in terms of large scale renovations or new cities (Adam Greenfield - “Against the Smart City”)
  • Plurality of scenarios: Pre-developed / developing urban contexts + Smaller cities/towns/villages (Ayona Datta - “Learning from small cities”)
  • Critical urbanism (Henri Lefebvre - “the right to the city”)
  • Another Smart City is Possible (Rob Kitchin)
  • Rethinking the Smart City (Francesca Bria + Evgeny Morozov)
  • Technological sovereignty (Alex Hache + Maxigas)
